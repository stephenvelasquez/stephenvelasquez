## Stephen Velasquez

I build products that work — and I build them across domains. Fifteen years shipping software at Amazon, Walmart, Microsoft, and the US Department of the Treasury, spanning supply chain, edge compute, fintech, defense tech, healthcare, AI, and platform infrastructure.

What my Microsoft coworkers said they valued most: **range**. I've led products in game engine simulation, defense-grade mesh networking, treasury financial systems, and cloud infrastructure — each requiring different technical depth, different stakeholders, and different definitions of "done." That range compounds. Every domain I've shipped in makes the next one faster and the decisions sharper.

At Microsoft I was dual-hatted across business and engineering for public and private market infrastructure, shipped Unity-based simulation products, and built edge compute systems for defense. At Amazon and Walmart I owned supply chain products where latency is measured in trucks, not milliseconds. At Treasury I led fintech product and engineering as a director. Every role reinforced the same lesson: the best technical product leaders don't just write specs — they write code, read data, and make hard calls when the room is split.

Now I run [ZipHealthy](https://www.ziphealthy.com), a behavioral health and consulting practice in Northwest Arkansas. I open-source the tools and frameworks I actually use.
